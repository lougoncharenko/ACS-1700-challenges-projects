Nutrition and Recovery

Why is nutrition important for Jujitsu?

Being that Jujitsu is a demanding sport, nutrition plays an important role in fueling the body and aiding in recovery. Just like with any other sports, Jujitsu athletes gain a competitive edge when they focus on fueling their body with nutritious foods that are full of vitamins and minerals. I have found that eating nutrient dense foods has helped me remain light on my feet and has given me greater focus. Once I started training, I cut out fried foods, processed foods, sugary drinks and even alcohol as consuming such things caused me to feel sluggish and tired. I have learned that I perform best when I eat well.

Proper Hydration

Staying hydrated is probably the single-most important thing any athlete can do. Jujtisu athletes are sweating throughout their workouts and need to be replenishing all the fluids they lose during sessions. I usually bring a liter of water to my training sessions and can drink the whole container during my workout. Being properly hydrated allows me to focus on my techniques and strategy whereas, when I am thirsty, often my performance suffers. Being hydrated helps regulate body temperature and lubriacates the joints, two things that are vital to staying alert and agile while grappling. Being dehydrated puts Jujitsu athletes at risk for muscles cramps, dizziness and fatigue.

Recovery techniques for Jujitsu

Pushing your body to the limit as one does in Jujitsu is strenous on the muscles and joints. To prevent injuries and to recover properly, I have developed some handy tools that I use to recover after my training sessions. The tips I have for recovering properly include:

  1. Stretch before and after a training session
  2. Get to class early to do a light warm up of the muscles via cardio
  3. Drink plenty of water before, during and after the session
  4. Get a good amount of rest and sleep during the day (8-10 hours
  5. take a rest day if your body is tired or injured.
  6. Eat a nutritious diet to help muscle tissue recover
  7. Use ice baths and saunas needed.
